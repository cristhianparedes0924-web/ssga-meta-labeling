Section 04 — Strategy Deep Dive

What Goes Into Primary Joubert+QuantAtoZ

Layer-by-layer breakdown of the strategy pipeline — from clean cross-asset data to the directional side signal and probability score.

  • 1Input layer: Clean monthly cross-asset data (SPX, commodities, rates, credit) with timestamp alignment and forward target construction.
  • 2Indicator layer: Momentum, cross-asset spread, rate change, credit carry, volatility regime, and drawdown features.
  • 3Robustness layer: Orientation-consistent normalization and outlier control to keep features comparable across regimes.
  • 4Combination layer: Correlation-aware weighting to avoid double-counting highly similar signals.
  • 5Output layer: Directional side plus continuous probability score for threshold-driven activation.

The wave view explains the Hilbert-style intuition: cyclical indicators move in phases, and the model interprets their relative phase alignment as risk-on / risk-off regime context.

Section 06 — Conclusion

Key Takeaways

Summary of backtest findings and their implications for the M2 meta-labeling filter layer.

Backtest Result

Moderate Risk-Adjusted Performance

Primary Joubert+QuantAtoZ produced 8.74% annualized return with Sharpe 0.587 and max drawdown −48.46%. The high SPX correlation (0.984) suggests limited independent timing value in the current form.

Classification Strength

Best F1 Score & High Recall

With F1 of 79.28% and recall of 98.73%, this model excels at capturing directional opportunities. M2 can leverage this by filtering low-confidence signals rather than increasing recall.

M2 Implications

Good Opportunity Generator for M2

This primary layer can still serve as an opportunity generator. M2 should focus on confidence filtering to reduce the −48.46% drawdown exposure and improve the active information ratio.
